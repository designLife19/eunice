Overview

SavingMate is that financial budgeting app that helps users plan and set short-term financial goals and allows users to easily set a goal and prioritize goals, adjust them as circumstances change, and receive personalized recommendations for saving. Users can track progress through visual aids, celebrate small victories, and categorize goals by priority by providing a simple and easy-to-use mobile platform.

This is an independent student project completed while in the UX Bootcamp program at the Brain Station. And I created this entire app from the scratch to the end as a UX/UI designer. It took 10 weeks to be completed.

Design Process

Problem Statement

Many young adults in Canada struggle to understand the importance of managing a budget and lack the skills and knowledge necessary to effectively budget, spend, and save their money. This can lead to financial difficulties such as debt, inability to reach financial goals, and insufficient savings for emergencies and future expenses.

Secondary Research

I conducted secondary research after defining the problem space for gathering existing knowledge and insights on the topic or issue at hand.

  • Nearly 90% of graduate students say they experience financial stress and anxiety.
  • Nearly one-third considered dropping out of school because of financial problems.
  • Still, 58% of Canadians under 30 have a goal to save as much money as possible in 2021, and 59% want to save even better.

Secondary Research Report Pie Chart

Assumptions

  • Canadians aged 18-30 are more likely to experience financial stress than those in other age groups.
  • Many young Canadians struggle with saving money and setting financial goals, and the pandemic has made financial planning more difficult for them.
  • Despite the majority of young Canadians experiencing financial stress over living costs, there may be a lack of awareness about financial resources and tools that can help them manage their money better.
  • There is a gap between young adults' intentions to create a budget and their actual behaviour in regularly reviewing and adjusting their budgets.

User Interview

Participants must:

  • Age: 18-30
  • Budgeting experience:
    - who have some level of experience with budgeting, including those who have attempted to budget in the past.
    - who are currently budgeting.
    - who have never tried but are interested in learning more.
  • Employment status: employed full-time, part-time, self-employed, or unemployed
  • Financial situation: who are debt-free, who have debt such as student loans, and who are in a precarious financial situation.
  • Attitudes towards budgeting: varying attitudes towards budgeting, including those who find it difficult or tedious, who enjoy it, and who are neutral.

Hightlight Select Theme
Short-Term Goals: Several young adults mention the importance of setting short-term goals that are achievable and provide a sense of progress and accomplishment. This may be a helpful approach to overcoming the challenges with planning and motivation.

Insight
Many young adults find it easier to achieve short-term goals, and the sense of accomplishment and progress that comes with achieving these goals can be motivating. Short-term goals can also help individuals avoid feeling overwhelmed and keep their focus on what they can accomplish in the short term.

Challenges

The main challenge of this project was to design an app that could assist users in setting and achieving short-term financial goals while also providing the motivation to stay on track.
Through plenty of research, user interviews, persona, and user testing, I understood the goals, motivations, and pain points of the users within their services as well as resolved any problems by designing a better solution for them.

Therefore, I assumed that if the app calculates the amounts and displays in visualization how much a user needs to save money per day, week, or month, it can help them to motivate to save money to achieve the goal user has set. There are some main points I should focus on;
  • Simplifying the process of setting goals
  • Making it easy to adjust goals and priorities
  • Providing clear and intuitive visualizations to track progress towards goals
  • Streamlining the progress report screen to make it easy to understand progress at a glance
  • Offering encouraging feedback or celebrating small victories to help users stay motivated and achieve their goals.

Solving

1. Intuitive interface:
Developed a user-friendly interface that allows users to set their goals quickly and easily. The interface should be visually appealing and simple to navigate, with clear instructions and prompts to guide users through each step. And Users should also be able to customize their goals' frequency, duration, and difficulty based on their individual needs and preferences.

Solution 01. Created a user-friendly interface for setting goals

2. Flexibility:
Build flexibility into the goal-setting process so users can easily adjust their goals and priorities. I used a drag-and-drop function to easy to adjust the priorities of goals.

Solution 02. Making it easy to adjust goals and priorities Developing clear and engaging visualizations to track progress

3. Visual goal representation:
Used visual elements, such as progress bars, charts, or graphs, to represent each user's goals. These visual representations should be easy to understand, visually appealing, and provide users with a quick overview of their progress towards their goals.

Solution 03. Developing clear and engaging visualizations to track progress

4. Clear and concise data:
Provide users with clear and concise data on their progress towards their goals. The progress report screen should include relevant information, such as the goal amount, progress made to date, and remaining balance in visual elements.

Solution 04. Designing a clean and concise progress report screen that allows users to quickly grasp their progress towards their goals

5. Personalized messaging:
Implemented features to encourage users to stay motivated and celebrate their achievements along the way. Provided personalized messaging to users based on user's progress towards their goals. This could include congratulatory messages when users reach significant milestones or encouraging messages to help users stay motivated when progress is slow.

Solution 05. Offering encouraging feedback or celebrating small victories to help users stay motivated and achieve their goals.

Results

With the solutions above, I articulated and simplified the SavingMate app's main features of providing a convenient setting of short-term financial goals, adjusting priorities, and easy tracking progress service through designed visuals.
01. Created a user-friendly interface for setting goals and adjusting priorities
02. Developing clear and engaging visualizations to track progress
03. Designing a clean and concise progress report screen that allows users to grasp their progress toward their goals quickly
04. Implemented features to encourage users to stay motivated and celebrate their achievements.

Key Learnings

And through this project, here are some key learnings.
01.Conducting user research, including interviews and creating personas, helped me understand my target users' needs, pain points, and motivations.
02.Creating low to high-fidelity wireframes and prototypes, and testing them with users, helped me identify usability issues, iterate on designs, and refine the user experience.
03.I learned that one of the main benefits of creating a UI library is ensuring consistency across different application parts. And also, measuring redlines is a crucial part of ensuring that my designs are pixel-perfect and that developers can implement them accurately.
